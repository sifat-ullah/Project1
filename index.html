Notice board

Maulana Bhasani announced the establishment of the university at the Kagmari conference on 8 February 1957, but organizational activities did not start until 1970. Despite his efforts, the Islamic University was not implemented during his lifetime. In 1982, a government initiative led to the formation of a committee to establish a university based on Bhasani's vision. However, due to political instability, progress stalled. In 1996, the government aimed to expand science and technology education in remote areas, and in 1997, with ADB support, plans were made to establish a science and technology university in Tangail. The foundation stone of Maulana Bhasani Science and Technology University (MBSTU) was laid by Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina in 1999. The university began classes on 25 October 2003 with two departments: Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) and Information and Communication Technology (ICT). Over the years, more departments were added, including Environmental Science, Textile Engineering, Biotechnology, and Business Administration. Between 2013 and 2015, the university faced significant unrest, including violence and vandalism, leading to a temporary closure in 2015 after the murder of a student leader.

The university began classes on 25 October 2003 with two departments: Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) and Information and Communication Technology (ICT). Over the years, more departments were added, including Environmental Science, Textile Engineering, Biotechnology, and Business Administration.
